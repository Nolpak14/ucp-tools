What is UCP?

The Universal Commerce Protocol is the new standard that lets AI agents shop on your store. Here's why it matters.

The Problem UCP Solves

AI assistants like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot are becoming the new way people shop. But there's a problem: AI agents can't easily find or buy from most online stores.

Without a standard protocol, each AI platform would need custom integrations with every store. That doesn't scale.

UCP is like robots.txt for commerce. Just as robots.txt tells search engines how to crawl your site, UCP tells AI agents how to shop on your store.

Who Created UCP?

UCP was developed by Google and Shopify, with endorsement from 60+ major companies:

Google Shopify Walmart Target Stripe PayPal Visa Mastercard Best Buy Home Depot Etsy Wayfair

How Does UCP Work?

Merchants publish a JSON file at a standard location:

https://yourstore.com/.well-known/ucp

This file tells AI agents:

  • What capabilities your store supports (checkout, orders, fulfillment)
  • How to connect (API endpoints, authentication)
  • What version of UCP you use

Why Should You Care?

AI-powered shopping is growing fast:

  • $20.9 billion in AI-assisted purchases expected in 2026
  • ChatGPT checkout already live with Target, Walmart, Etsy
  • Google AI Mode launching with UCP integration
  • Microsoft Copilot Checkout auto-enrolling Shopify stores
If you don't have UCP, AI agents will skip your store and send customers to competitors who do.

UCP vs. Other Protocols

UCP isn't the only protocol. OpenAI has the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP) with Stripe. But UCP has more momentum with Google, Shopify, and 60+ partners backing it.

Smart merchants will implement both, but UCP is the priority for now.
